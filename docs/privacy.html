Wusel Capture — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 14 July 2026 · Applies to the Wusel Capture browser extension and its optional native messaging host.

The short version. Wusel Capture has no servers, no backend, no accounts, no analytics and no telemetry. Your screenshots and page data are processed entirely on your own machine and are never transmitted to us. We are incapable of receiving them.

The one exception you should understand: when you choose to send a capture to your AI coding assistant, that assistant is a separate product with its own privacy policy. See §4.

2. What the extension handles

The extension does nothing until you click its toolbar icon and take a capture. When you do, it assembles a capture on your device from the following:

  • A screenshot of the page — the visible viewport, the full page, or a region you draw. Images only. No video, no audio, no camera and no microphone access.
  • Page context — the page URL and title, your browser's user agent, viewport size, device pixel ratio, scroll position, screen size, browser name and version, platform and language.
  • Diagnostics — a rolling in-memory buffer of the most recent console errors/warnings and failed network requests on the page, so your assistant can see what went wrong. For failed requests we record only the URL, method, status and timestamp. We never record request or response bodies, HTTP headers, cookies, or the contents of page storage.
  • Your own input — the note you type and the annotations you draw (text, arrows, boxes, redaction).

What it does not do

The extension does not log keystrokes, clicks, or mouse movement; does not read your browsing history, bookmarks, cookies or passwords; does not read the page's DOM text or form values; and does not track you across sites. It observes only the page you explicitly capture.

3. Where a capture goes

Nowhere near us. A finished capture takes one of two local paths, both of which you choose:

  • Download (default). The annotated image is saved to your own disk through the browser's normal "Save file" dialog. Your assistant is then opened via a vscode:// or codex:// link pointing at that local file.
  • Native host (optional). If you deliberately install our companion host, the capture is written straight into the matching project folder on your disk, and your locally installed editor is opened on it. The host is open source, runs entirely on your machine, and makes no network connections.

4. Your AI assistant is a third party

Please read this section carefully — it is the only way capture data can leave your machine.

The whole point of Wusel Capture is to hand your annotated screenshot to an AI coding assistant such as Claude Code (Anthropic) or Codex (OpenAI). Those are separate products, installed by you, running under your own account. When you send a capture, that assistant will transmit the image and your note to its vendor's servers in order to answer you.

That transmission is between you and your assistant's vendor, under their privacy policy and terms — not ours. We are not a party to it and never see the data. If your capture contains something sensitive, it will be sent to that vendor just like anything else you paste into your assistant. Use the built-in Redact tool (see §6) before sending.

5. Storage and retention

Where What Retention
IndexedDB (local) Pending captures, purely to hand a capture from the popup to the editor tab. Kept locally because a full-page screenshot is too large for extension storage. Automatically pruned to the 10 most recent captures.
chrome.storage.local Exactly two UI preferences: which assistant you picked, and an optional Claude Code session id you may type in. No images, no page content, no URLs. Until you change or clear them.
Your disk Captures you chose to save or send. Yours. Delete them whenever you like.

Uninstalling the extension removes everything it stored in the browser. Files you already saved to disk remain, because they are yours.

6. Redaction

Two protections are built in, and both apply before a capture is exported or sent:

  • The Redact tool pixelates any region of the image you mark. The pixelation is baked into the exported bitmap, so the original pixels are not recoverable from the file that leaves the editor.
  • Automatic URL redaction. Query parameters in captured request URLs whose names look credential-like (token, secret, password, authorization, cookie, session, jwt, api_key, access) have their values replaced with [REDACTED].

These are safety nets, not guarantees. A screenshot shows whatever was on your screen — review it before you send it.

7. Why the extension asks for each permission

Permission Why
tabs To identify the tab you are capturing, take the viewport screenshot, and open the annotation editor.
scripting To collect the technical page context (URL, title, viewport, scroll) for the tab you captured.
debugger The only way Chrome can screenshot content below the fold. Used solely for full-page capture, attached and detached around that single screenshot. No other DevTools Protocol domains are enabled.
downloads To save the annotated image to your disk, and to learn the resulting file path so your editor can be pointed at it.
storage To remember the two UI preferences described above.
nativeMessaging To hand a capture to the optional local companion host, if you installed it.
<all_urls> Because the page you want to capture can be any page — localhost, staging, or production. A fixed list of sites would simply mean the tool fails where you need it. Access is used only on the tab you capture.

8. Selling, sharing and tracking

We do not sell or transfer your data to anyone, because we never receive it. We do not use it for advertising, profiling, credit scoring or lending decisions. There are no third-party analytics, trackers, tag managers or advertising SDKs in the extension, and it loads no remote code — everything it runs is bundled in the package you install.

9. Your rights

Data-protection law (including the GDPR) gives you rights of access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection and portability over personal data a controller holds about you. Wusel Capture holds none: the data stays on your device and under your control, so there is nothing for us to disclose, correct or erase. You can exercise full control at any time by deleting your saved captures or uninstalling the extension.

If you believe we have nonetheless mishandled your data, contact us at wusel-capture@wusel.app. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority.

10. Children

Wusel Capture is a developer tool and is not directed at children. It collects no data from anyone, of any age.

11. Changes to this policy

If the extension's behaviour ever changes in a way that affects your privacy, this policy will be updated before that version ships, and the "last updated" date above will change. The revision history is public in the Git repository.
